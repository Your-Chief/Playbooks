# Playbooks & Chief: README Summary

## What Are Playbooks?

Playbooks are **human-readable, open-source operating guides** that define how teams and organizations run. They're written in plain English (Markdown), not code, making them accessible to everyone—executives, operators, and individual contributors alike. 

Think of a playbook as a repeatable operating model: a daily standup format, a PRD template, an onboarding checklist, a performance review process, or an entire stack of how your company operates. They document not just *what* you do, but *how* and *why*.

## What is Chief?

**Chief turns intent into execution.**

**[Chief](https://Chief.bot)** is the command layer for founders and operators at fast-growing companies. It is not a task manager, a search tool, or a document assistant. It is an AI chief of staff — an operating system that takes your vision, coordinates the work required to achieve it, and keeps you ahead of what's happening before you have to ask.

Most tools help you organize information. Chief does something different: it runs the coordination so you can run the company.

And Cheif runs on these Playbooks.

### Chief Runs on Playbooks

This is where it comes together.

Chief is governed by **Playbooks** — clear, human-readable instructions for how work gets done. Playbooks define how your day is planned, how meetings are prepared, how follow-ups happen, how blocked work is addressed, how decisions are documented, and how priorities are revisited.

Playbooks are written in plain English. They are visible, editable, and written in plain language. There are no hidden rules. When something needs to change, you change the playbook — not the people.

Playbooks are layered and composable:

| Layer | Example |
|---|---|
| **Personal** | How you like to work, review, and delegate |
| **Project** | How this specific initiative runs |
| **Team** | Shared norms, cadences, and communication standards |
| **Org** | Company-wide standards and operating principles |

Chief knows how to inherit, compose, and execute across all of these layers simultaneously — adapting to your company's specific context while still running on community-validated best practices.

**Playbooks define the model. Chief runs it.** That's the key distinction: the open-source Playbooks repository is where operating best practices live and evolve. Chief is the system that knows how to execute them — understanding your team's context, tracking status in real time, coordinating agents across your work, and keeping you ahead of the airplane.

## How Playbooks Work with Chief

 Chief is an action-oriented agentic operating system that executes playbooks. Here's the relationship:

- **Playbooks define the structure:** Your playbooks codify best practices and repeatable processes.
- **Chief runs them intelligently:** Chief reads your playbooks, understands the intent, and coordinates execution across your team. It manages ownership, status, dependencies, and provides daily briefings based on what's defined in your playbooks.
- **No Notion required:** Rather than manually maintaining playbooks in Notion or Google Docs, you upload, trade and remix them on Chief. Chief extracts the intent and guides your team's work around those principles.

Example: You have a "Weekly Review" playbook that specifies which metrics to review, who owns each section, and what decisions need to happen. Chief runs that playbook every week, automatically gathering data, surfacing the key numbers, and routing decisions to the right person.

## The Core Vision: Share, Remix, and Scale Best Practices

 We believe the best operating practices should be shared, not locked away. This repository enables a "worldwide explosion of sharing and remixing of playbook stacks" where:

- **You fork playbooks from others.** Download a PRD template used by top product teams, a hiring playbook refined by 50 companies, or an entire operational stack built for early-stage startups.
- **You remix them for your context.** Adapt, modify, and layer playbooks together to build your perfect operating system.
- **You contribute improvements back.** Found a better way to run your daily standup? Improved a template that works for your industry? Submit a PR. Over time, playbooks improve through community iteration—becoming "33 generations deep from the world's best minds."

 The composability model mirrors Git: fork repos, branch playbooks, issue PRs to improve individual plays, and merge improvements back into the main corpus. Communities eventually swap entire playbook stacks that run their companies.

## Why Open Source?

 Playbooks are plain text. They should be open source. There's no proprietary magic in how you run your standup or structure your PRD—the magic is in getting everyone to follow it consistently and improving it over time based on what actually works. And that's where Chief really shines, becuase it turns your intent into execution.

Open-source playbooks:
- **Reduce friction:** Anyone can adopt, fork, and modify without lock-in.
- **Build moats through execution, not restriction:** Chief's value isn't in keeping playbooks private—it's in running them intelligently, remembering what worked, and coordinating your team around those patterns.
- **Enable the community to validate best practices:** Over time, the most effective playbooks rise to the top through ratings, contributions, and adoption.

## How to Contribute

We welcome PRs to improve playbooks. Here's how:

1. **Spot an improvement:** Found a better way to structure a playbook? See a gap that should be filled?
2. **Open an issue or submit a PR:** Propose the change with a clear rationale.
3. **Community review:** The community votes and discusses. Core maintainers and our playbook maestro agent ensure consistency across related playbooks.
4. **Merge & propagate:** When approved, your improvement merges in. Chief automatically notifies users (opt-in) of the update.

Each playbook tracks its **generation and lineage**—showing exactly which minds contributed to this version and what problems were solved along the way.
